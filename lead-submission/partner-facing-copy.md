# Partner-Facing Copy

## Module intro

Submit a business funding lead for review.

Use this form when a business owner has asked to explore possible funding next steps and has given permission for you to share their information with the Partner Command Center team.

This is not an approval form. This is an intake and review step.

## Submission instructions

Before submitting:

1. Confirm the business owner gave permission.
2. Provide accurate contact information.
3. Add the clearest business context you have.
4. Do not upload sensitive documents through this form.
5. Do not promise approval, funding amount, pricing, or timing.
6. Include notes if the lead is urgent, incomplete, or needs careful handling.

## Consent language

Use this language before submitting a lead:

> I confirm the business owner gave permission to submit this information for review and understands that submitting a lead does not guarantee approval, funding, terms, rates, or timing.

## What happens next

After submission:

1. The lead is checked for required information.
2. Consent and partner attribution are reviewed.
3. The lead may be routed to an admin review queue.
4. The business owner may be contacted for follow-up if appropriate.
5. Additional information may be requested.
6. A next step may be recommended based on the submitted profile.

No funding outcome is guaranteed.

## What not to promise

Do not tell applicants:

- “You are approved.”
- “You qualify for a specific amount.”
- “Everyone qualifies.”
- “This will not involve any review.”
- “No credit concerns matter.”
- “Funding is guaranteed.”
- “We can fix credit.”
- “Lenders are definitely ready to fund you.”

## Approved partner language

Use:

- “I can submit your info for review.”
- “The team can look at possible next steps.”
- “Funding options depend on the business profile and available documentation.”
- “There may be a follow-up request for more details.”
- “No approval or funding outcome is guaranteed.”

## FAQ

### Is this a funding application?

No. This is a partner-submitted lead intake form. It helps route the business owner to the right review path.

### Can I promise a funding amount?

No. Do not promise or imply a specific amount, approval, rate, term, or timeline.

### Should I submit documents?

No. Do not submit bank statements, tax returns, IDs, credit reports, SSNs, or sensitive documents through this static/demo module.

### What if I do not know all the details?

Submit only if you have consent and the minimum required information. If key fields are missing, mark the notes clearly.

### Can I submit leads from a purchased list?

No. Do not submit scraped, purchased, or cold lead data unless there is documented permission from the business owner.

### What if the business owner needs money immediately?

Submit accurate urgency context, but do not promise speed. Immediate urgency should trigger careful human review, not reckless claims.

### How do tracking IDs work?

Tracking IDs connect a lead to a partner and campaign. Batch 19 will define the tracking link builder.
